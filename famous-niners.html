All-Time Greats

Jerry Rice had 1,281 catches for 19,247 yards and scored 187 touchdowns in 238 games for the 49ers in his career. Rice won three Super Bowls (Super Bowl XXIII (’88), Super Bowl XXIV (’89) and SB XXIX (’94)) and one Super Bowl MVP. He currently holds 36 NFL records.

Joe Montana had a passer rating of 93.5 with 35,124 yards, 244 touchdowns and 123 interceptions in 167 games for the 49ers in his career. Montana started and won four Super Bowls and was the first player to be named the Super Bowl Most Valuable Player (MVP) three times.

Patrick Willis had 733 solo tackles, 217 assists, 20.5 sacks, 5 fumble recoveries and 8 interceptions in 112 games for the 49ers in his career. 7x Pro-Bowler and was nominated as All-Pro five times.

Drafted in the first round in 1981 NFL Draft, Lott became an important player for the 49ers. During his rookie year, he recorded seven interceptions, helping the 49ers win SuperBowl XVI. He finished his career as 49er with four rings and getting his number retired.

Steve Young had a passer rating of 101.4 with 29,907 yards, 221 touchdowns and 86 interceptions in 150 games for the 49ers in his career.
